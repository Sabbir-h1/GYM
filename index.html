    Fat Loss Engine

    CARDIO METHODS

    🏃
    Treadmill — HIIT
    Wednesday main session
    5 min easy walk warm-up → 8 rounds of (30s sprint at 80% max speed / 90s slow walk) → 5 min easy cooldown. Total ~25 min. Burns fat for hours after.
    🚶
    Treadmill — Incline Walk
    Sunday steady session
    Set incline to 8–12%, speed 5–6 km/h. Walk for 30–40 min steady. Incline walking burns almost as many calories as running with zero joint impact — perfect for 105 kg bodyweight.
    ⚡
    Cross Trainer
    Warm-up every strength day
    10 min steady pace before every strength session. Gets your heart rate up, warms your joints safely. Set resistance to moderate — you should be slightly breathless but able to talk.
    🔥
    Jumping Jacks
    No machine needed
    3 sets × 50 reps between strength sets to keep heart rate elevated. Burns extra calories without adding recovery time. Also works as a finisher after any session.
    💪
    Burpees
    No machine — high intensity
    Add 3 × 10 burpees at the end of Wednesday HIIT or Saturday leg day. Full body explosive movement — one of the highest calorie-burn exercises per minute. Start slow, build up.
    🏋️
    Mountain Climbers
    No machine — core + cardio
    3 × 30 seconds as a core finisher. Drive knees to chest rapidly in a plank position. Combines cardio with ab work — hits lower abs which are stubborn at high body fat.
    🦘
    Jump Rope Simulation
    No machine — anywhere
    Even without a rope, jump rope motion (small hops, wrist rotation) burns ~10 cal/min. 3 × 1 min rounds between sets. Low impact once you get the rhythm — great for Wednesday HIIT warmup.
    🧘
    High Knees
    No machine — warm-up option
    30 seconds of driving knees up to hip height rapidly. Use as an alternative warm-up if cross trainer is busy. Also great as active rest between sets to keep calorie burn high.

    Pro tip

    As a beginner at 105 kg — incline treadmill walking is your best friend. Running puts 3× your bodyweight on joints. Incline walking gives you 80% of the calorie burn with none of the joint stress. Increase incline every 2 weeks as you get lighter and fitter.

    Non-Negotiables

    THE RULES

    01
    Progressive overload every week
    Add slightly more weight or 1 extra rep every 1–2 weeks. Your muscles only grow when challenged more than last time. Write your weights in a notebook every session.
    02
    Form before weight — always
    At 16, your joints and tendons are still developing. Use a weight you can control for all reps with full range of motion. Bad form with heavy weight equals injury, not muscle.
    03
    Sleep 8–9 hours minimum
    Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep at your age. You literally build muscle and burn fat while sleeping. Under 7 hours will destroy results no matter how hard you train.
    04
    No deadlift or bench press
    Your spine is still maturing. Leg press, goblet squats, machine chest press and cable work give 90% of the same benefit with far lower injury risk at your age and weight.
    05
    Protein at every single meal
    Muscle is built from protein. Under 110g daily and you'll lose muscle while losing fat — called skinny fat. Eggs, chicken, fish, milk, dal are your best budget sources.
    06
    Weigh yourself once a week only
    Every Sunday morning, before eating, same conditions. Daily weighing is misleading due to water fluctuation. Track the weekly trend, not the daily number.
